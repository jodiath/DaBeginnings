1.    HAVE FUN.  There are MANY rules in golf and they tend to change.  So, if there are any questions or concerns about the rules then work with your opponents to decide the best course of action, mark the scorecard and continue play.  Once the match is complete, inform the captains after the match.


2.     Men will hit from the White Tees (exception being hole 15 where they will hit from the red) and Women will hit from the Red Tees.


3.     If a team's drive (both playing partners) goes into the hazard (trees, water or Out of Bounds - not counting sand traps) then there are three options:
 - Both teams decide where the balls entered the hazard and then the team in the hazard chooses the ball they want to use and puts a mark down on the ground.  Then the team in the hazard gets two club lengths from the mark to drop their balls and continue play. (NO PENALTY STROKE)
 - The team in the hazard finds a lie, in the fairway (at the 150 yard marker for par 4's, 200 yard marker for par 5's), and gets a club length on both sides to hit its next team shot. (ONE PENALTY STROKE)


4.     For par three's and every other lie after the tee shot, if a team's ball comes to rest in a hazard (both players hit into the hazard - not counting sand traps), then both teams decide where the balls entered the hazard and then the team in the hazard chooses the ball they want to use and puts a mark down on the ground.  Then the team in the hazard gets two club lengths from the mark to drop their balls and continue play. (NO PENALTY STROKE)
(please, do not spend more than 2 minutes searching for a ball, in the hazard, as this may cause play to be delayed.)
 - For Example:
    Team A hits both tee shots on a par three into the hazard (one in the woods and one in the water)
    Team A and Team B first decide the point the balls entered the hazard then Team A chooses the one they want to play.
    Next Team A marks the ground where the chosen ball entered the hazard
    From this point Team A gets to place their balls within two club length of the mark in the ground and then proceed with play.  (NO PENALTY STROKE)


5.     The maximum score a team can record for a hole is triple bogey (three over par).  If both teams are at triple bogey on a hole and neither ball has been holed out then the hole shall be considered HALVED and both teams should gather their balls and proceed to the next hole.
 
Bonuses:
1. Each team in a match, during each session, will have one team mulligan.  When used the team must inform their opponent that they are using a mulligan.  Then the team taking the mulligan can no longer use the last two balls they hit but MUST take either of the next two balls that are hit.  Mulligans are per team not per person.
 - For Example:
    Team B hits both their shots into the hazard.
    Team B tells TEAM A they are using their team mulligan.  This nullifies the first two balls.
    Team B then drops their balls and hits again and proceeds with play.
