Format

The format is Ryder Cup style. 

We are playing this format in the form of (2) 9 hole super ball sessions.  This means that all opponents are separated into two BIG teams with each BIG team having a captain.  These BIG teams are then set up into two person pairings.  These pairings then compete, using match play format, to win points for their BIG team.  each pairing can either defeat their opponents and gain their BIG team ONE point or they can tie, or HALVE, their opponent thus giving both BIG teams a HALF point each. To win, one BIG team needs to attain a minimum of FIVE points. In the case of a tie, the winner will be decided by the captains picking a paring, from the last session, they want to use and a third party picking one of the last sessions holes.  The pairing with the lowest score wins a HALF point.  Thus that BIG team will win.

The Captain is the person in charge of the BIG team.  His or her duties are as follows:
Pick the players for his/her team from the hat. (first pick will be determined by the toss of a coin)
Set the line-ups for his/her Ryder cup team for each session.

Submit team name
Is the final decision on any disputes concerning his/her team.

Sessions are defined as a single nine holes of golf played by all pairings from both teams with scores being tallied and recorded.  There will be three nine hole sessions played.

 - The line up for the first session will be turned in first by the captain who received the first draw from the hat.

 - The line up for the second session will be turned in first by the captain who got the second draw from the hat.

 - The line up for the third session will be turned in by both captains together.

This is a Super Ball scramble.  A Super ball scramble means that both people on the same team hit a shot, and then they decide which of the two shots offers the best next shot.  After the decision is made, a marker of some sort (tee usually) is stuck in the ground marking the lie and each ball is placed, in turn, within a club length of the marker on either side.  Then both players proceed to hit again.  The process is repeated till the team has a ball in the bottom of the cup.  NOTE: When the ball is on the green, not the fringe, instead of a club length from the mark the ball can only be placed a putter head distance on either side of the mark.

This is MATCHPLAY.  This means that score is kept by holes not strokes.  If one team scores lower on the hole than the other then that team wins the hole.  This is represented by stating the winning team is "One UP', denoting the winning team is one hole up on their opponent.  This marking method is continued as holes are won (Two Up, Three Up, etc.). Following this same process, the team that lost the hole would be represented by stating they are "One Down".  If both teams tie a hole then that is stated as "The Hole is Halved".  The match is considered complete when one team is up more holes than are left to physically play.

For Example:

Team A and team B are playing a nine hole match.
Team A scores a lower score on hole one than team B.
This is recorded as team A is "One UP" and team B is "One Down".
On the second Hole, team A scores lower than team B again.
Now, this is recorded as team A is "Two Up" and team B is "Two Down".
Holes three through seven both teams make equal scores on every hole so they have "Halved" these holes, HOWEVER team A is still up by Two Holes (they won holes one and two). This is represented at team A is "Two up with Two Holes to play".
At this point, the match is considered to be dormie.  Dormie means that team B can not beat Team A in the match.  Team B can only tie the match or "Halve the Match".  To halve the match, team B would need to score lower than team A on each of the remaining two holes.
On hole eight, team A scores lower than team B. This means that team A won the hole and is now "Three Holes Up".  Since team A is up three holes and there is only one hole left to play, team A has won the match.
The win is represented as "Team A won the match Three Up with One to Play" or "Team A won Three and One".

When the match is complete, meaning one team has won more holes than the other, then both teams shall proceed to the clubhouse and report their scores.  This may occur before a full nine holes is played.  That is OK and will help to speed play and prepare for the next session.
