Rules

1. Have fun.  There are many rules in golf and they tend to change.  So, if there are any questions or concerns about the rules then work with your opponents to decide the best course of action, mark the scorecard and continue play.  Once the match is complete, inform the captains.

2. Men will hit from the White Tees and Women will hit from the Burgundy Tees.

3. If a team’s drive (both playing partners) goes into the hazard (trees, water or out of bounds - not counting sand traps) then drop at the 150 yard marker for par four’s, and at the 200 yard marker for par five’s (ONE PENALTY STROKE)

4. For par three’s and every other lie after the tee shot, if a team’s ball comes to rest in a hazard (both players hit into the hazard - not counting sand traps), then both teams decide where the balls entered the hazard and then the team in the hazard chooses the ball they want to play and puts a mark down on the ground. Then the team in the hazard gets two club lengths from the mark to drop their balls and continue play. Alternately if there is a drop area for the hole the team that is in the hazard may drop in the drop area.  (NO PENALTY STROKE) Note: One tree a line does not make!  

(Please, do not spend more than ONE minute searching for a ball, in hazards, woods, or out of bounds, as this will cause play to be delayed.)

For Example:

  • Team A hits both tee shots on a par three into the hazard (one in the woods and one in the water)
  • Team A and Team B first decide the point at which the balls entered the hazard then Team A chooses the one they want to play.
  • Next Team A marks the ground where the chosen ball entered the hazard
  • From this point Team A gets to place their balls within two club lengths of the mark on the ground and play continues.  (NO PENALTY STROKE)

5. The maximum score a team can record for a hole is triple bogey (three over par) this includes your handicap.  If both teams are at triple bogey on a hole and neither ball has been holed out then the hole shall be considered HALVED and both teams should gather their balls and proceed to the next hole.


Bonuses

1. Each two persons/pairing on the same team in a match, during each round, they will have one mulligan.  When used, they must inform their opponent that they are using a mulligan.  Then the team taking the mulligan can no longer use the last two balls they hit but MUST take either of the next two balls that are hit.  Mulligans are per two people pairing not per person.

For Example:

  • Team B hits both their shots into the hazard.
  • Team B tells Team A they are using their team mulligan.  This nullifies the first two balls.
  • Team B then drops their balls and hits again and play continues.
